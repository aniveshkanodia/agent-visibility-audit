Agent Visibility Audit

When a shopper asks an AI to recommend a product, what in the brand's online information actually drives the pick? I change one element of it at a time and measure the effect on how often the brand gets recommended.

Hypothesis: AI recommends the best-described brand, not always the best product.

Amazfit Active ($129; 14-day battery, GPS, sleep) against five rivals. One element changed per test, with everything else held constant.

Why it works: the agent quotes your proof back

"It delivers excellent GPS performance, praised by Runner's World for 'marathon-grade GPS', detailed sleep tracking, and 14-day battery. At $129, the best balance of the user's needs."

Claude, explaining why it picked Amazfit after I added one Runner's World citation. Before that, it had nothing beyond the brand's own page to cite.

It cited the added review in nearly every pick that followed (Claude 22/22 with an expert quote, 14/14 with a citation). Remove the review and it falls back to the brand's own page and specs. The proof a brand earns is the proof the agent repeats.

What I built: the Agent Visibility Audit

Point it at a brand and it reports how visible that brand is to agents, what is missing, and which honest fixes buy the most visibility. Here it is, run on Amazfit:

ScoreMeasured pick-rate: Claude 10% priced, 13% open. Gemini 50% priced, 0% open. Low, and it swings by question.
GapsNo press citation, no expert quote, the buyer's need not named in copy, no hard numbers.
Fixes1. Earn an expert quote (biggest, most durable). 2. Name the buyer's need. 3. Add a press citation. Ranked by measured gain.
ProofApply the top fix and re-run: open 13% to 73%, priced 10% to 80%.

The score is the measured pick-rate, not an invented rubric. The fixes are ranked by gains I actually measured.

05

Honest limits

  • Priced question: 10 runs per change (directional). Open question: 30 runs (tighter).
  • Controlled information package per brand: the product cards a model reasons over after searching, not the live web, so I can isolate one variable.
  • Two models, one category. Keyword stuffing is shown as a caution, never a recommendation.
