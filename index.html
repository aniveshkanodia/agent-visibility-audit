Agent Visibility Audit

When a shopper asks an AI to recommend a product, what in the brand's online information actually drives the pick? We change one footprint element at a time and measure it.

Hypothesis: AI recommends the best-described brand, not always the best product.

Amazfit Active ($129 — 14-day battery, GPS, sleep) vs. five rivals. One element changed per test; everything else held constant.

Two models decide differently

On the open question, Claude swings from 13% to 73% depending on how Amazfit is described — an expert quote wins.

Footprint quality moves the evidence-driven model against a stronger rival.

Gemini stays near 0% no matter what you change; it keeps picking Garmin, the Runner's World Editor's Choice.

With a price limit, Gemini loved Amazfit — that was price fit, not pages.

03

What the AI reaches for

When we added press or expert coverage, the AI cited it in nearly every run where it picked Amazfit (Claude 14/14 and 22/22 on the open question; 8/8 and 9/9 with price). Without it, the model fell back to the brand's own page and specs.

04

Honest limits

  • Priced question: 10 runs per change (directional). Open question: 30 runs (tighter CIs).
  • Controlled information package per brand — post-retrieval product cards, not live web — to isolate one variable.
  • Two models, one category. Keyword stuffing shown as caution, never a recommendation.
