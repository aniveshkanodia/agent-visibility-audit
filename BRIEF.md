# Competition Brief — PMG: Brand Visibility & Discovery in the Age of Agents
*AI & Tech Sandbox Global Hackathon 2026. Source: `references/Brand Visibility and Discovery in the Age of Agents - Participant Brief.pdf`.*

## The opportunity
When a consumer delegates a decision to a personal AI agent ("find me a running shoe
for flat feet under $150"), the agent doesn't browse or click ads. It reasons over
whatever information it can access and recommends a brand on behalf of someone who may
never see the intermediate steps. Open questions nobody has rigorously answered:

1. What causes an agent to recommend one brand over another?
2. What information does an agent actually reach for — and what does it ignore?
3. What are the signals that build agent-side trust and confidence in a recommendation?
4. How does a brand's representation across the web (structure, sources, coverage)
   translate into agent-era visibility?
5. What is the equivalent of search rank / quality score / share of voice when agents
   are the audience?
6. Can you reliably predict when and why an agent will recommend a brand — and what
   would change that prediction?

## The challenge
Pick a real brand and category. Design a set of controlled information environments.
Run real AI agents through them. Find out what actually influences agent recommendations
— and build something that gives a brand a clear answer and a specific action. It's a
research challenge as much as a build challenge. The deliverable is findings, not infrastructure.

## The method (fixed)
- **Agents already exist** — ChatGPT, Claude, Perplexity, Gemini are live and API-accessible. Use the real ones.
- **You build the environments** — a controlled information context = a curated set of
  inputs representing what an agent can access about a brand + its competitive landscape.
- **Isolate the driver** — by varying environments and holding the query constant.
- What dimensions to vary, how many to test, what to measure = your design (part of the challenge).

## Your tools
- **Data — you source it.** No provisioned datasets. Curating/structuring/presenting public
  brand information across environments is the design work at the heart of the challenge.
- **Agents — they already exist.** Use real ones; findings quality depends on experimental design.
- **Tooling — your call.** Judges won't reward infrastructure complexity, only sharp experiments.

## What judges evaluate
- **Question quality** — a question about brand performance in the agent world worth answering.
- **Experimental design** — environments that actually isolate what you're testing.
- **Finding quality** — specific, surprising, grounded in real agent behavior.
- **Deliverable usefulness** — a CMO / strategist / agency lead would find it valuable.
- **Originality** — an angle nobody else in the room would take.

## Required deliverables
1. **Cover slide** — team name, country, project title, 15–20 word description, key image.
2. **Deck (5–10 slides)** — hypothesis → experimental design → findings → what brands should do.
3. **Video demo** — narrated walkthrough of experiments and findings in action.
4. **Video testimonial (optional)** — 30–60s on the hackathon experience.

## Rules
- All output must be brand-safe and appropriate for a professional judging context.
- All deliverables submitted before the final presentation window.
